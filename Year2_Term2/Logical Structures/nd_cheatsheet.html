STUCK

Goal is A → B

Use →i. Open a subproof, assume A, prove B, close the box.

Goal is ¬A

Use ¬i. Open a subproof, assume A, derive ⊥, close the box.

Goal is A ∧ B

Use ∧i. Prove A and B separately, then combine them.

Goal is A ∨ B

Use ∨i&sub1; if you can prove A, or ∨i&sub2; if you can prove B. If neither is obvious, try PBC.

Goal is ∀x P(x)

Use ∀i. Open a subproof, pick fresh a, prove P(a), close the box.

Goal is ∃x P(x)

Use ∃i. Find a concrete term t, prove P(t), then generalise.

You have A ∨ B

Use ∨e. Handle both cases: subproof 1 assumes A, subproof 2 assumes B. Both must reach the same conclusion C.

You have ∃x P(x)

Use ∃e. Open a subproof, assume P(a) for fresh a, derive your conclusion C (must not contain a), close the box.

You have ∀x P(x)

Use ∀e freely. Instantiate to any term you need. You can apply it multiple times with different terms.

You have A and ¬A

Use ¬e to get ⊥, then ⊥e to derive whatever your goal is.

Completely stuck

Try PBC: assume ¬(goal), derive ⊥, conclude goal. Or use LEM on a relevant formula and case-split.
